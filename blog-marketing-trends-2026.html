Marketing trends that matter.

What is actually changing in MENA marketing in 2026, from AI-assisted content and conversational commerce to trust-first branding, and what is just noise.

Manara ConsultancyAdvisory · Beirut & the Gulf
7 MIN READ
Share

Every year brings a fresh list of marketing trends, most of which are noise. Here are the shifts we see genuinely changing how MENA businesses win attention in 2026, and how to use them without chasing hype.

01 Conversational commerce grows up

Customers increasingly want to ask, decide and buy inside a chat. WhatsApp-first journeys, backed by AI agents that answer instantly in Arabic, English and French, are becoming the default for service businesses, not a novelty.

02 AI-assisted, human-approved content

The winning teams use AI to plan and draft at scale, then apply human judgement to voice and taste. The losers publish unedited output and sound like everyone else.

03 Trust as the differentiator

  • Clear pricing and honest scope over clever slogans
  • Real proof and case detail over stock enthusiasm
  • Consistency across channels that signals reliability
  • Privacy and confidentiality treated as features

04 What to ignore

Platform-of-the-month panic and vanity metrics. Depth on the two or three channels where your customers actually are will beat a thin presence everywhere, every time.

05 Localisation beats translation

Running the same campaign through a translation tool is not localisation. The businesses winning attention in MENA speak to each market in its own register — the Arabic that feels native rather than converted, the reference that lands locally, the offer shaped to how people in that country actually buy. A message that reads as written-for-here always outperforms one that reads as translated-for-anywhere.

06 Owned channels over rented audiences

Followers on a platform are borrowed; the platform sets the rules and can change them overnight. The lasting asset is an audience you own — an email list, a WhatsApp broadcast, a base of past customers you can reach directly. Social builds the relationship; owned channels keep it. The businesses that treated their followers as the whole strategy are the ones most exposed when a platform's reach collapses.

How to actually adopt a trend

The point of a trend list is not to chase all of it. Pick the one or two shifts that match where your customers already are, commit to them properly for a quarter, and ignore the rest without guilt. Depth on the right channel beats a thin, anxious presence across every new thing. Trends reward focus, not frenzy.
