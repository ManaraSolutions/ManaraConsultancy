4 · Choose the right route to market

How your product reaches the buyer changes everything about cost, control and margin. We pick the route the market actually rewards.

Direct

Producer → Consumer

  • Full control and margin, higher effort to reach and serve
Via retail

Producer → Retailer → Consumer

  • Reach and shelf presence, shared margin and negotiation
Via wholesale

Producer → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer

  • Widest coverage, least control over the final sale
Fragmented market

Many small players

  • Modern trade, wholesalers and groceries mixed
  • High complexity, distribution challenges, varied credit terms
  • Lower retailer power — but higher set-up cost
Consolidated market

A few dominate

  • Modern trade takes the vast majority of volume
  • Lower complexity, easier coverage — segments set by the retailer
  • High retailer power, margin passing to the retailer

We read where a market sits on that spectrum — and adapt the go-to-market strategy accordingly.
